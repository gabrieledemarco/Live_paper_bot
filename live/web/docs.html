Research note  ·  June 2026

A regime-gated order-flow strategy on BTC/USDT perp, and what we honestly know about its edge.

The model deployed in this paper account is the v2.1 configuration that survived a walk-forward + Monte Carlo permutation test on BTC (p = 0.014, 5/5 OOS windows positive). Live trading here is the forward test the offline pipeline could not produce. This page documents the data, the procedure, the model, and the strategy — including what we don't know.

Data

Everything is built on free, public Binance market data. The live model uses USD‑M perpetual futures (ticker BTCUSDT), with three streams stitched on the same one-minute timeline:

OHLCV
1 m / 5 m / 15 m / 60 m candles, 180-day rolling window (~259k base bars), via ccxt binanceusdm.
Order flow
Raw klines including taker-buy volume (CVD), funding rate (8h), perp−spot basis. From the raw fapi endpoint and fetchFundingRateHistory.
Live feed
Public websocket wss://fstream.binance.com/ws/btcusdt@kline_1m; no API keys, no rate-limit exposure.

ETH was tested with the identical pipeline. The walk-forward permutation p = 0.889 there: we kept it out of production.

Procedure — how we got here

The current model is the third iteration. The first two failed; we kept them visible to make this credible.

  1. v1  ·  baseline

    LightGBM direction classifier on multi-timeframe OHLCV proxies (44 features), tercile labels at t+5min. ROC-AUC ~ 0.62 in-sample, but on the cost-aware backtest all six configs lost hard (−65% to −93% on validation). Reason: the label was direction, the strategy traded barriers, and stops fired ~2× more than targets.

  2. v2  ·  aligned + selective + cost-realistic

    Two binary classifiers P(win|long) and P(win|short), trained on triple-barrier outcomes (= the actual TP-before-SL the strategy will trade). Entry threshold, SL and TP are jointly Bayesian-optimised on net Sharpe. Maker entries, taker exits, leverage 2×, confidence-scaled sizing. Result: near break-even (BTC −1.0%, ETH −0.1%) — the bleed of v1 was gone.

  3. v2.1  ·  regime gate  +  order flow

    We tagged every v2 validation trade by 60-minute regime cell (volatility tercile × trend sign). PnL was clearly regime-structured: BTC's edge lives in trend-up / mid­-vol; ETH's in high­-vol. Then we added free order-flow features (CVD, funding, basis) and re-ran the rigorous test. BTC's permutation p-value dropped from 0.63 → 0.014; all 5 walk-forward windows positive; ETH stayed null. This configuration is what the paper account runs.

The model

Two independent LightGBM classifiers wrapped in a sklearn StandardScaler pipeline. Each predicts the probability that a hypothetical long (resp. short) entry at the current minute close would hit a fixed reference take-profit before its stop within the next 30 bars. 

P_long  = model_long.predict_proba(features_t)[:, 1]
P_short = model_short.predict_proba(features_t)[:, 1]

The labels were generated with conservative reference barriers (ref_sl=15 bps, ref_tp=30 bps) — we deliberately decoupled training labels from execution barriers, so the Bayesian optimiser can pick execution SL/TP without forcing a retrain.

Features (44 columns)

  • Price / volume — close-vwap deviation, returns, range, volume z-score, volume std.
  • Microstructure proxies — volume imbalance, OBI proxy, trade-flow proxy, Garman-Klass and Parkinson volatility, Amihud illiquidity.
  • Multi-timeframe — the same blocks resampled on 5m, 15m, 60m, merged with strict causal alignment.
  • Order flow (added in Phase 1) — cumulative volume delta (CVD), CVD slope & z-score, taker-flow imbalance, funding rate & change, perp-spot basis & change.

The strategy

The model is the easy part. Translating its probabilities into trades correctly is what saved v2 from being v1 with extra steps.

1  ·  directional decision

thr  = 0.748              # entry_threshold (BO-optimised)
if   P_long  >= thr and P_long  > P_short: side = +1
elif P_short >= thr and P_short > P_long:  side = -1
else                                       side =  0

2  ·  regime gate

Volatility tercile (realised vol over the last 24 hours on 60m bars) × trend sign (close vs EMA-24 on 60m) defines six cells. The trade is taken only if the current cell is in the favorable_cells set found on the optimisation segment. 

favorable_cells = ['low_up', 'mid_down', 'mid_up']
if cell not in favorable_cells: side = 0     # gate blocks

3  ·  confidence-scaled sizing

p_chosen   = P_long if side == +1 else P_short
size_frac  = clip( (p_chosen - thr) / (1 - thr), 0, 1 )
notional   = equity × leverage × size_frac

So a marginal signal (p = 0.75) sizes ≈ 0; a strong signal (p = 0.90) sizes ≈ 60% of full notional. We never bet the farm on a borderline call.

SL / TP

Time-stop

Fees
maker 2 bps, taker 4 bps — Binance USD-M public schedule.
Funding

Statistical validation

We deliberately did not stop at a single in-sample backtest. The 4 bps cost barrier is thin enough that any apparent edge has to survive a battery of tests before we trust it.

TestBTCETH
Single-window OOS return +0.23% −0.07%
Walk-forward, 5 windows — pooled OOS +3.53% −1.21%
Windows positive 5 / 5 1 / 5
Monte Carlo P(positive total) 99% 12%
Permutation test p-value 0.014 0.889

Methodology, in plain words:

  • 3-way split with embargo: train (60%), opt (20%), validation (20%). Models fit on train; SL/TP/threshold and favorable cells chosen on opt; performance measured on validation. None of those three pieces ever see each other's data.
  • Walk-forward: that same 3-way split, rolled over 5 anchored windows, with regime cells re-selected per window. Every validation block is strictly out-of-sample.
  • Monte Carlo bootstrap on pooled OOS trade returns: 2000 resamples with replacement → distribution of terminal return, percentiles, P(positive).
  • Permutation test: random sign-flip on the same trade returns, 2000 draws. The p-value is the fraction of permuted means that match or beat the actual. p = 0.014 means there is a 1.4% chance of seeing this edge by luck under the null.

Honest caveats

This is the part most strategy pitches skip. We won't.

  • One pair survived, not two. A universal edge would have moved ETH too. BTC may be exploiting a structure that does not generalise.
  • One historical window. The walk-forward is rolling within a single 180-day period. The strategy has not seen 2020-style vol or 2022-style attrition.
  • Multiple-testing tax. We tried ∼20+ configurations across the program. p = 0.014 on the winner is meaningfully weaker once corrected. The Deflated Sharpe per-bar comes out near zero, which we attribute to the strategy being sparse (the per-bar Sharpe denominator is tiny). The per-trade permutation test stays the more relevant measure here.
  • Free order flow only. The strongest microstructure signal in the literature is multi-level OFI from L2 depth (Kolm et al. 2023). That needs paid history (Tardis / Kaiko). We chose to find out whether free flow was already enough — it is, marginally, on BTC.
  • Fills are simulated. Our maker-limit orders do not actually exist on the venue. Real fills depend on queue position; in practice some of the "filled" trades here would have missed. This is what makes paper paper.
  • Leverage 2× amplifies mistakes. One adverse SL costs ~6.5 bps of equity, not 3.2. The path to ruin is shorter than gross numbers suggest.

VERDICT Promising, not proven. Paper trading is the actual forward test; we will judge after 30–60 days of out-of-time fills, not after 30 minutes.
